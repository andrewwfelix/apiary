Structuring your affiliate categories into "clusters" is a great way to build high-intent pages that convert. Given your interest in technical specs (like your Trek bicycle component knowledge) and history (The Odyssey/Carthage), we can create some really unique angles.

Here are the proposed categories for The Apiary Guide:

1. The Library (Educational Resources)
The Beginner’s Shelf: Core "how-to" books (e.g., Beekeeping for Dummies, The Beekeeper’s Handbook).

Historical & Literary Honey: Books on ancient beekeeping (Carthaginian/Greek methods) and nature writing.

Technical Manuals: Advanced queen rearing and hive biology texts.

2. The Wardrobe (Protective Gear)
Armor & Suits: Full ventilated suits vs. jackets.

The Essentials: Gloves (goatskin vs. nitrile) and veils/hats.

Kid-Sized Gear: Family-focused beekeeping apparel.

3. The Hive Body (Structural Components)
Foundation & Frames: Technical comparisons (Beeswax-coated plastic vs. 100% natural wax).

The Boxes: Deep vs. Medium supers, 8-frame vs. 10-frame configurations.

Specialty Hives: Flow Hives, Top Bar hives, and Warre hives.

4. The Tool Belt (Maintenance & Inspection)
The "Big Three": Smokers (and fuels), hive tools (J-hooks vs. standard), and bee brushes.

Technical Gadgets: Hive scales, digital thermometers, and infrared cameras for winter monitoring.

Feeding Supplies: Internal frame feeders, entrance feeders, and winter patties.

5. The Harvest Lab (Extraction & Processing)
The Extractor Room: Manual vs. electric centrifugal extractors.

Uncapping Station: Hot knives, scratcher forks, and uncapping tanks.

Bottling & Storage: Glass jars, skep-style containers, and custom labeling kits.

6. The Apothecary (Bee Health & Wellness)
Pest Management: Varroa mite treatments (oxalic acid vaporizers, Apivar).

Winterization: Hive wraps, moisture boards, and windbreaks.

7. The Gift Shop (Lifestyle & Home)
Beeswax Crafts: Candle-making kits, silicone molds, and DIY lip balm supplies.

Honey Lover Gifts: Honey dippers, specialized flight tastings, and bee-themed home decor.

My Opinion:
Since we’re scaling to 100 pages, Category 4 (The Tool Belt) and Category 2 (The Wardrobe) are your "workhorses"—these items have high search volume and frequent replacement needs. However, Category 1 (The Library) is where you can lean into your specific expertise in classical literature to build a unique "Historical Beekeeping" niche that no one else is touching.
