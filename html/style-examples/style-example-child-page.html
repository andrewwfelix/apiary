What we check

Every corner of your site,
scrutinised.

Our audit covers six technical areas that directly influence how search engines crawl, index, and rank your pages — each mapped to specific fixes.

01
Crawlability
We verify that search engines can access every important page — checking robots.txt, noindex tags, and crawl directives that might be blocking your content.
Screaming Frog
02
Broken links & redirects
A full map of every 4xx error and redirect chain on your site. We fix broken links and clean up multi-hop redirects that waste crawl budget and lose link equity.
Screaming Frog
03
Page speed & Core Web Vitals
We test LCP, CLS, and INP on mobile and desktop against Google's thresholds. Failing Core Web Vitals is a direct ranking disadvantage — we fix it.
Search Console · Netlify
04
On-page elements
Audit of every title tag, meta description, H1, and canonical tag across your site — flagging duplicates, missing values, and tags that are too short or too long.
Screaming Frog · Semrush
05
Indexing & sitemap
We cross-reference your sitemap against Google's index to find pages that should be indexed but aren't — and pages that are indexed but shouldn't be.
Search Console
06
Internal linking structure
We map your internal link graph to identify orphaned pages, high click-depth content, and missed opportunities to pass authority to your most important pages.
Screaming Frog · Semrush

The process

How an audit
actually works.

From first crawl to verified fixes — here's the exact sequence, with no surprises.

01
Baseline crawl
We run a full Screaming Frog crawl of your site and record your current health score as the official starting point.
02
Issue mapping
Every error is categorised, cross-referenced with Search Console data, and ranked by its impact on your rankings.
03
Fix & deploy
Fixes are implemented via GitHub pull requests and deployed through Netlify — with a preview environment before anything goes live.
04
Verify & report
We re-crawl, confirm improvements in Search Console, and deliver your before/after report. Invoice follows on proof.
